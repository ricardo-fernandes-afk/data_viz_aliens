They Are Among Us?

A Data Story by The Freaking Aliens

When the Sky Went Crazy

Sightings surged in waves—perhaps reflecting cultural moments, not cosmic arrivals.

Aliens Love Summer Nights

Most sightings occur in summer and after dark—coincidence?

Hotspots on Earth

The majority of sightings come from the US. But not all states are equal: California, Florida and Texas lead the list.

Triangles, Fireballs and Lights

In the 60s, classic discs dominated. Today, it's fireballs, lights and triangles. Are aliens upgrading their vehicles—or are we just describing what we expect to see?

Blink and They're Gone?

Most sightings are short—many last less than 5 minutes. But some UFO types linger longer than others.

Aliens and Holidays

Sightings peak on Halloween, New Year’s and the Fourth of July. Maybe aliens love fireworks—or they just like to party.
